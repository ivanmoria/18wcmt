About this Work

Abstract

Introduction

This study maps the scientific panorama of the 17th World Congress of Music Therapy (held in 2023 in Canada) through a bibliometric analysis of its proceedings. Our aim was to identify main research themes, leading authors and collaboration networks, based on one possibility for collecting and analyzing the rich material presented at this congress.

Methods

A manually extracted dataset from the proceedings book was used to analyze data from 238 published abstracts. Each study was categorized by research design and target population, allowing for a quantitative overview of prevailing research trends in the field. Descriptive statistics and co-authorship network analysis were conducted using the bibliometrix R-tool (Aria & Cuccurullo, 2017).

Results

Clements-Cortés A emerged as a leading author. Co-authorship analysis revealed five main collaboration clusters, with Chan V, Mercadal-Brotons M, and Zanini C as key connectors across groups. Analysis of target populations showed that the most represented group was the Music Therapy Community and related professionals (83 studies), followed by studies with a Gerontological perspective (29). Studies about mental health and children were also significant, each nearing 25. Regarding study designs, experience reports and clinical practice studies were the most prominent, totaling 87. Among theoretical and clinical studies, approximately 50% were qualitative, 12% quantitative, and 38% used mixed methods.

Discussion / Conclusion

This study provided an overview of collaboration networks, key research themes and connections between groups, helping to understand the scientific dynamics of the music therapy community. The prominence of experience reports reflects the field's strong commitment to applied knowledge, while the diversity in study designs underscores its methodological pluralism. These insights can guide future research and foster strategic partnerships, broadening the field's impact and interdisciplinarity.
